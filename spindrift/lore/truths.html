Cataclysm

The Sun Plague extinguished the stars in our home galaxy.

Likely caused by the blooming of a supermassive black hole that rent the space and time of all it touched, the anomaly traveled at incredible speeds and snuffed out the stars around us before we could formulate an answer.

Powered by untested technology, we scrambled to send a fleet of starships to the one point on our galactic maps that we had any chance of reaching with enough time to spare: the Forge. But we lost all contact with the Genesis fleet long before it reached its destination. Though tasked with sending back a signal to assure the rest of us they had reached a new home, no such communication ever came.

As the stars surrounding our homeworld continued to blink out and the void drew ever closer, we made the decision to launch a second fleet, this one carrying all that remained of humanity as our Sun dimmed to apocalyptic night. With no idea if we'd be welcomed into safety or find only the cold remains of our brothers and sisters, we followed in their footsteps.

But here in the Forge, the stars are still aflame.

We were greeted by the descendants of the people aboard the Genesis fleet, strange alien hybrids called the Aliaj. The species who presumably met the first of us to arrive are long gone. Not even the Aliaj remember their progenitor race, or so they say.

Exodus

Two ragtag fleets of ships—propelled at tremendous speeds by experimental FTL drives—carried our ancestors to the Forge.

But the technology that powered the ships is said to be the source of the Sundering, a fracturing of time and reality that still plagues us and is the reason for the Exodus's arrival so long after the Genesis, despite the journeys barely being a decade apart. Following in the wake of such power caused relative time to behave in unexpected ways, and by the time the Exodus finally arrived in the Forge, a hundred years or more had passed.

The experimental drives used by the Genesis & Exodus fleets are forbidden, but the damage is done. The Sundering spreads across our reality like cracks on the surface of an icy pond. Those fissures unleash even more perilous realities upon our own. Did we flee one cataclysm, only to inadvertently create another?

Communities

We have made our mark in this galaxy, but the energy storms we call balefires threaten to undo that progress, leaving our communities isolated and vulnerable.

Starships navigate along bustling trade routes between settlements. We've built burgeoning outposts on the fringes of known sectors, and bold spacers chart new paths into unexplored domains. But this hard-earned success is threatened by the chaotic balefires, intense energy anomalies that cut off trade routes and threaten entire planets.

The Aliaj have never been accepted by the rest of humanity. They are far outnumbered, and so far seem uninterested in taking back territory that has been stolen for them in the centuries since the Exodus arrived. They've largely been pushed into the Outlands, distrusted by humans wary of their strange alien powers and biology. Those of the Aliaj who can pass as human often do, as life in The Forge is hard enough without the stigma of unknown alien ancestry.

Iron

Iron vows are sworn upon totems crafted from the enigmatic metal we call black iron.

Black iron was perhaps first forged by the civilization that bore the Aliaj. Some say it is a living metal, attuned to the hidden depths of the universe. Remnants of this prized resource are found within ancient sites throughout the Forge. Naski especially seem to sense its resonance and are occasionally, begrudgingly, hired by grubs and archeologists on expeditions to seek it out. It is resistant to damage and corrosion, but can be molded using superheated plasma at specialized facilities. The Ironsworn carry weapons, armor, or tokens crafted from black iron, and swear vows upon it.

Laws

Laws and governance vary across settled domains, but bounty hunters are given wide latitude to pursue their contracts. Their authority is almost universally recognized, and supersedes local laws.

Through tradition and influence, bounty hunter guilds are given free rein to track and capture fugitives in most settled places. Only the foolish stand between a determined bounty hunter and their target.

Religion

Our faith is as diverse as our people.

Many have no religion, or offer an occasional prayer out of habit. Others pay homage to the gods of our forebears as a way of connecting to their roots. Some idealize the natural order of the universe, and see the divine in the gravitational dance of stars or the complex mechanisms of a planetary ecosystem. Fewer still worship The Twins, a hypergiant star and black hole that orbit each other at the center of the cluster, somehow perfectly balanced. These are the Gods of the Aliaj and their forebears; it is almost unheard of for humans to acknowledge them as anything other than a curious natural phenomenon.

Magic

Unnatural energies flow through the Forge. Magic and science are two sides of the same coin.

Soon after our arrival, we discovered that the Aliaj displayed the uncanny ability to harness the Forge's energies. After the passing of generations here, humans too began to display such power, though it is comparatively much rarer and weaker.

Today, mystics invoke these energies to manipulate matter or see beyond the veils of our own universe. But this can be a corrupting force, and the most powerful mystics are respected and feared in equal measure. For the same reasons, and because the Aliaj have a much deeper connection to the Forge, they are looked upon with even deeper suspicion.

Communication and Data

In settled domains, a network of data hubs called the Warp allow near-instantaneous communication and data-sharing between ships and outposts.

Computers and personal devices that connected to the Warp are called weft machines, or wefts, for short. A.I. programs that exist within this network are called weavers.

Because of their importance, Warp hubs are often targets for sabotage, and communication blackouts are not uncommon. Beyond the most populous sectors, travelers and outposts are still commonly isolated and entirely off the grid.

Medicine

To help offset a scarcity of medical supplies and knowledge, the resourceful technicians we call riggers create basic organ and limb replacements.

Much was lost in the Exodus, and what remains of our medical technologies and expertise is co-opted by the privileged and powerful. For most, advanced medical care is out of reach. When someone suffers a grievous injury, they'll often turn to a rigger for a makeshift mechanical solution.

Artificial Intelligence

The vestiges of advanced machine intelligence are coveted and wielded by those in power.

Much of our AI technology was lost in the Exodus. What remains is largely under the control of powerful organizations and people, and is often wielded as a weapon or deterrent. The rest of us must make do with primitive systems, though most starships are equipped with a simple onboard A.I.

War

Professional soldiers defend or expand the holdings of those who are able to pay. The rest of us are on our own.

Mercenary guilds wield power in the Forge. Some are scrappy outfits of no more than a dozen soldiers. Others are sector-spanning enterprises deploying legions of skilled fighting forces and fleets of powerful starships. Most hold no loyalty except to the highest bidder.

Lifeforms

This is a perilous and often inhospitable galaxy, but life finds a way.

Life in the Forge is diverse. Planets are often home to a vast array of creatures, and our starships cruise with spaceborne lifeforms riding their wake. Even animals from our homeworld—carried aboard the Exodus ships—have adapted to live with us in the Forge.

Aside from the Aliaj and humanity, however, we have yet to discover any intelligent life still extant within the Forge. All evidence of civilizations that came before us suggests they are here no longer.

Precursors

Over eons, a vast number of civilizations rose and fell within the Forge.

Today, the folk we call grubs—scavenger crews and audacious explorers—delve into the mysterious monuments and ruins of those ancient beings.

Incomprehensible technologies, inexorable time, and the strange energies of the Forge have corrupted the vaults of the precursors. Despite the perils, grubs scour those places for useful resources and discoveries. But some secrets are best left buried, and many have been lost to the forsaken depths of the vaults.

Horrors

Put enough alcohol in a spacer, and they’ll tell you stories of ghost ships crewed by vengeful undead. It’s nonsense.

Within the Forge, space and time are as mutable and unstable as a flooding river. When reality can't be trusted, we are bound to encounter unsettling phenomenon.
